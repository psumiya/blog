Serverless Architectures
12/10/2016

Before we know what it is, it is easier to clarify what it is not. Contrary to its name, Serverless does not really mean there are no servers. The intent of serverless architectures is to transfer the operational burden of running and maintaining servers where your code runs to your chosen cloud provider.

This is still a rapidly evolving space, but I reckon we will start to see the industry coalesce around this idea in the next decade (so, by the end of 2025). Essentially, my prediction is that by the end of 2025, containers (Kubernetes, Docker, et al) would have reached their decline stage in the product maturity chart and teams would be hard pressed to answer why serverless is not a cheaper and faster alternative to buidling distributed systems at scale.
